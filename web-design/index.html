About Me

Hi, I'm Robert Guzman from Jupiter, Florida. My journey in creative arts is fueled by a desire to merge eye-catching visuals with interactive experiences, particularly in games and media. My skillset isn't limited to artistic abilities, I'm also versed in various programming languages, a combination I've nurtured at UCF, known for its comprehensive tech and business programs.

I'm a Digital Media student at UCF, where my coursework and projects have allowed me to explore and blend technology with creative expression. The university's rich, diverse research topics and its strong emphasis on tech and business have been instrumental in shaping my multidimensional skills.

Art caught my interest during my teen years, a passion that has only grown over time. My portfolio is filled with pieces mainly created with traditional media, like ink and chalk, characterized by bold, minimalistic designs. Each artwork reflects my grounding in basic design principles and my growing interest in weaving narrative into visual art.

In the realm of game development, I'm particularly interested in game programming. I imagine contributing to the creation of open-world RPGs that are not just games but experiences, where storytelling and gameplay intersect. Each line of code and design element is a step closer to bringing imaginative worlds to life.

The culmination of my educational and creative journey is to contribute to a team that breathes life into expansive, interactive worlds. Witnessing a blend of technology and art that offers players unforgettable, immersive experiences is the milestone I'm aiming for.

Inspirations

One of my major sources of inspiration is Jamie Hewlett, a renowned English comic book artist and designer, best known for co-creating the virtual band Gorillaz. Hewlett's unique style, characterized by a combination of British comic book aesthetics and Japanese anime influences, has had a significant impact on my artistic journey. His ability to merge art and music, storytelling, and character design inspires me to explore the multidimensional aspects of visual art.

My artistic senses have also been profoundly influenced by Grace Liu, an incredible concept artist and illustrator whose work is distinguished by its vibrant colours and intricate details. Liu's artworks, teeming with life and energy, embody a captivating blend of Western and Eastern art traditions. Her remarkable ability to breathe life into every stroke and color inspires me to push boundaries, infusing my creations with a soul and character that transcend the canvas.

Another pivotal figure in shaping my artistic inclination is Tite Kubo, the esteemed Japanese manga artist, celebrated for creating the iconic 'Bleach' series. Kubo's distinctive style, marked by its sleek lines and dramatic shading, carries the profound essence of classic manga while introducing innovative elements. Every panel crafted by Kubo is a testament to his storytelling prowess, encouraging me to intertwine narrative and visual art in a dance that speaks to the soul."
